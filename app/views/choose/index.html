Choose

User Needs

As a visitor I need to know the difference between visas so that I can make a choice when I have more than one option

Evidence:

User research

UKVI services on GOV.UK

Researchers: Clare Ridd and Joe Coker
Participants: 12 interviews with visit visa holders
Date: October 2017

Choosing a visa is easier when there is a close match between activity and type of visa. Someone coming to do a training course with their company got confused as to whether this was came under a business visit or a student visit:

  • "I was a little bit confused between a business visa and the visa given for student doing a short-term course".

    • Another was not clear whether their business trip constituted work and they needed to go to the full effort of a work visa:

  • "there is another visa I think takes more time and effort to get…I needed something simpler because I was not going to work"

    • Conversely, it is easier when close match between activity and type of visa. Well named visas help with this:

  • "The names of the choices, when you try they look a bit more convenient"

    • Some users had read all the visas and chosen theirs by a process of elimination:

  • "It was not so quickly to know which visa I needed… finally I got standard visa"]; "I looked at all the other visas and I did not fall into any of the categories … in the end I followed by instincts and went for the tourist visa" [R6:P12]

    • As a visitor I need to know all the costs of the visa before applying so that I can plan how I will save the money

    Evidence:

    User research

    UKVI services on GOV.UK

    Researchers: Clare Ridd and Joe Coker
    Participants: 12 interviews with visit visa holders
    Date: October 2017

    Users described instances of confusion and frustration when surprised with unexpected costs during the application process. Some of this frustration arose as users felt they understood what the cost of the visa would be and had saved accordingly. They had not accounted for this hidden cost in their plans and were only made aware of it once they had started the application

    • "because there's an English test you have to do, sorry. So, I didn't let it cost money. That is not on the website. Or it might be there, but I didn't see it…, I was then told that I have to pay for the test, and it's close to £200, but I think £180 sounds like, oh my god, and I didn't know that I had to pay this much for the test (R6 -P11)
    • "So, in planning, I was planning for the visa fee, I was planning for the medical fee, and I knew where to do the medical fee from (R6 - P11)
    • "When we had doubts about a question or two we tried to call the support centre, the UK support centre or something like that, but it costs about £5 or something like that for questions to be answered (R6 - P7)

    For the hidden costs the user had to spend considerably more time investigating so they felt comfortable they knew what was expected of them

    • " So, it took me about two hours to actually get all the information that I needed. Whereas, for the medical, it took me less than five minutes. (R6 - P11)
