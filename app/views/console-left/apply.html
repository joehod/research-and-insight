It is not clear what documents and evidence users are required to submit during the visit visa application.

Impact:

Users will seek guidance from third parties such as friends or travel agents on which documents to prepare and submit.

Unclear document requirements mean that users tend to include everything the could think of.

Much of the documentation users submit is of little value and wastes time, effort, money and attention

There are large costs involved in shipping, storing and processing this documentation.

Evidence:

User research

UKVI services on GOV.UK

Researchers: Clare Ridd and Joe Coker
Participants: 12 interviews with visit visa holders
Date: October 2017

Several users described their confusion over which documents to provide:

  • "It's not easy for any kind of visa"
  • "now again I hand those in because I don't know exactly what"
  • "what exactly they want you to hand in. Because I might hand in totally irrelevant documents"
  • "So I think it is different and we might need more supporting docs"
  • "Somesupporting documents and I didn't know what those documents should be"

Users often had to ask a third party for guidance over what to supply:

  • "So, I have to ask a tourism company and they instructed how to prepare the documents" R6 - P3)
  • The first time I applied I asked from friends who had applied before what they did, and of course they said, "For proof of residence you need to hand in… all of them were based on judgemental, and based on what friends had told me, based on also what they had used to hand in (R6 - Arthur)

China field research

Researcher: Katy Arnold
Participants: 18 interviews with chinese nationals
Date: 2014

We should work much harder to make it clear that documents are supposed to support the online application. This isn't obvious to our users who don't seem to make a very strong connection between 'the form' and 'documents'. Users feel uncertain about which documents to supply.

The documents you supply should give evidence for the statements you have made in the application itself - seems to have been lost in the noise. We don't give much advice on which documents to supply within our app, though we do give some structure on the 'cover sheet' (which users print off).

I couldn't detect any awareness that the one was supposed to inform the other. The documents provide evidence for the application (form), which is obvious to everybody 'in the business' but less obvious to users.
