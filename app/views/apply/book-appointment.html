    Evidence:

    User research

    UKVI services on GOV.UK

    Researchers: Clare Ridd and Joe Coker
    Participants: 12 interviews with visit visa holders
    Date: October 2017

    Users gave a strong sense that they were not always clear exactly what was needed when a question was asked during the application:

    “what information you exactly need to put in" [P10]

    "So I think the only thing I’d like to think about is have a help sheet like that, like a, guidance sheet so you know when the person is filling, okay for this. What is the answer? How do you fill this? This is how you fill this.” [P7]

    “I would still say a filled example form on the website” [P9]

    “But the second time it’s easy, it was just not very difficult because I have example on it” [P2]

    “To be more precise, they asked me for the CAS number, C-A-S. I never met before that kind of requirement. It is a number, practically, that the university must give, that number. It is some kind of number that proves that you are unconditionally accepted on your study course, but, unfortunately, they did not provide me with that CAS number. That CAS number is, practically, for the Tier 4 Visa, and not for short-term student visitor visa, but I realised that later. In the first moment, when I applied for the visa, when I started to fill out these online forms, “Do you have CAS number?” “Uh-oh!” I asked my friend what to do now. He said, “Tell him the truth. Do you have it? No, you don’t. Tell him the truth,” and I said, “Yes.
